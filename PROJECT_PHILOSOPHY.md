# Project Philosophy

Q-MATIS is built on a set of core scientific principles that guide every architectural decision, every line of code, and every data structure.

## 1. Science is cumulative
We build on the past; we never erase it. The history of materials science is a history of iterative improvements. Q-MATIS is designed to permanently preserve the entire history of its own discovery process, enabling future generations of AI to learn from decades of accumulated experiments.

## 2. Negative results are valuable
Knowing what doesn't work is required to learn what does. When a machine learning model generates a crystal candidate and rejects it due to structural instability, or when a DFT calculation fails to converge, that negative data is mathematically and scientifically valuable. Q-MATIS preserves all failures, dead-ends, and rejections.

## 3. Nothing is overwritten
Data is strictly append-only. A prediction made today might be proven wrong tomorrow, but the fact that the prediction was made with a specific model version at a specific time is a historical fact that must not be deleted.

## 4. Every prediction is reproducible
Perfect state-tracking ensures we can rewind to any exact mathematical conclusion. Every prediction, substitution, and filtration event in Q-MATIS is tied to the exact Git commit, random seed, and configuration snapshot that generated it.

## 5. Every material has a permanent identity
Universally unique identifiers track materials across space and time. A material is not just a chemical formula; it is a specific structural configuration with a specific lineage.

## 6. AI should assist scientific discovery, not replace scientific reasoning
AI is a tool, not a substitute for physical constraints and human validation. Neural networks excel at pattern recognition, but they must be bounded by rigid physical laws (e.g., charge neutrality, oxidation states). Q-MATIS enforces these constraints via a Physics-Aware Discovery Engine before invoking any black-box AI.

## 7. Models evolve, but scientific memory persists
Machine learning architectures (CGCNN, ALIGNN, etc.) will eventually become outdated. Checkpoints decay in relevance. But the raw experimental truth—the Structures, the DFT validations, the physical properties—remains forever. The Scientific Memory Engine is the permanent foundation upon which all transient models are built.

## The Long-Term Vision
Q-MATIS is intended to grow over decades. Rather than solving one isolated scientific problem (like predicting superconductors), it aims to become permanent scientific infrastructure capable of supporting future AI systems, autonomous laboratories, and materials discovery across every scientific discipline.
